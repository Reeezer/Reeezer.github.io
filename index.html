Personnalized Spinal Cord Reconstruction @ .NeuroRestore

Autumn 2024

Developed a machine learning pipeline to generate pre-annotations for spinal root segmentation from MRI scans, supporting neuroprosthetic solutions for Spinal Cord Injury (SCI) patients. Leveraged nnU-Net for adaptable segmentation and HarDNet for efficient processing, addressing challenges such as limited data and closely packed spinal structures. Enhanced pre-annotation quality through advanced pre-processing, augmentation, and post-processing, demonstrating how AI can streamline personalized spinal cord reconstruction and reduce manual effort in clinical workflows.

Deep Learning Neuroimaging Fine-Tuning

Learning the Natural Language of the Spinal Cord @ .NeuroRestore

Spring - Autumn 2024

Pre-trained a 3D Masked Autoencoder based on Vision Transformers (ViT) to learn the underlying structure and language of the spinal cord from MRI data. This foundational model was fine-tuned for downstream tasks such as spinal cord segmentation and artefact removal, achieving improved performance and efficiency. The pre-training significantly enhanced fine-tuning on these tasks, reducing the need for large, annotated datasets, which are often costly and time-consuming to obtain in the medical field.

Deep Learning Neuroimaging Foundation Training Research

Based on META Masked Autoencoders
